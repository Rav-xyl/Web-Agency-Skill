# Cold Pitch & Prospecting Reference

---

## Research Workflow (do this before writing a single word)

When given any business info — name, address, map link, Instagram, description — run the full research pass before pitching.

### Step 1 — Verify no website
```
1. Google: "[Business Name] [City]" — does a domain appear in results?
2. Google Maps listing → website field populated?
3. Instagram bio → link present? (Linktree counts as "has a website")
4. JustDial / Sulekha / IndiaMART → website field blank?
5. Facebook page → About section → website field blank?

→ All blank = HIGH PRIORITY prospect
→ Only social = MEDIUM (they have digital presence, pitch an upgrade)
→ Has a site but it's terrible = MEDIUM (redesign angle)
```

### Step 2 — Deep research pass
```
Google Reviews:
- Total count: ___
- Average rating: ___
- Common praise themes: ___
- Common complaints: ___
- Recent review sentiment: ___

Social media:
- Platform used: ___
- Follower count: ___
- Posts per week: ___
- Engagement quality (comments, saves, shares): ___

Competitors in area:
- Do they have websites? ___
- Are any ranking on Google for "[service] [city]"? ___
- What do the best competitor sites look like? ___

Revenue signals:
- Premises: busy / premium / budget / unclear
- Pricing: premium / standard / budget
- Online ordering possible: yes / no
- Likely monthly revenue estimate: ___
```

### Step 3 — Build the pitch brief (before writing)
Answer these before drafting:
- What specific problem does NOT having a website cause THIS business?
- What would a website concretely add? (bookings, menu, portfolio, reviews, maps, e-commerce)
- What outcome can you promise in plain language? ("People searching 'best salon Indiranagar' can't find you")
- What's one piece of research that will impress them? (mention a specific review, their competitor's site, a local search volume)

## Pitch Message Templates

### WhatsApp / Instagram DM (short, warm)
```
Hi [Name], 

I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet — I found you on [Google Maps / Instagram] and was genuinely impressed by your [X reviews / beautiful product shots].

The problem is, most people searching "[your service] in [city]" on Google can't find you. That's customers going to [Competitor Name] instead.

I build websites for local businesses in [city] — clean, fast, gets you found on Google. 

Would it be okay if I sent you a quick example of what I'd build for [Business Name]?

— [Your name]
```

### Email pitch (slightly longer)
```
Subject: [Business Name] — quick question about your online presence

Hi [Name],

I came across [Business Name] while [researching salons in Koramangala / looking for the best cafes in HSR] — your [X reviews / reputation for Y] caught my attention.

I noticed you don't have a website yet. I checked Google: when someone searches "[your service] in [area]", [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] show up at the top. You're not there.

That's fixable. I build websites for local businesses — the kind that show up on Google and turn visitors into customers. For a business like yours, the main goals would be:
- Show up when people search "[service] [area]"
- Display your [menu / services / portfolio] professionally
- Let customers [book online / call directly / find directions]

I'd love to send you a quick concept (no cost, no commitment) so you can see what's possible.

Can I?

[Your name]
[Your website]
[Phone number]
```

### Redesign pitch (existing bad site)
```
Hi [Name],

I came across [Business Name]'s website while looking at [your industry] in [city]. You clearly have a quality business — your reviews back that up.

I checked your site on mobile, and it's not doing you justice. It [loads slowly / looks outdated / doesn't show up on Google / isn't mobile-friendly] — which means people landing on it are probably leaving before they call you.

I redesign sites for businesses like yours. The work I do typically means showing up higher on Google and converting more visitors into actual customers.

Worth a 10-minute call to show you what I'd change?

[Your name]
```

## Objection Handling

### "We don't need a website, we get business through referrals."
"That's great — referrals are the best kind of customer. But what happens when someone gets a referral and Googles you to check? If there's nothing there, or what they find looks unprofessional, you're losing some of those referrals before they ever contact you. A website is your 24/7 credibility check."

### "We have Instagram, that's enough."
"Instagram is great for discovery, but it doesn't help when someone searches Google. And Instagram is someone else's platform — if your account gets restricted or the algorithm changes, you have no control. Your website is yours forever. It also ranks on Google, which Instagram posts don't."

### "We tried a website before, it didn't do anything."
"That's really common — most cheap websites don't get any traffic because they're not set up for Google. What I do differently is build the site to rank from day one: proper structure, local SEO, page speed. I also set up analytics so you can actually see the results. Can I show you what that looks like?"

### "We can't afford it right now."
"I understand. Can I ask — roughly how many new customers would you need per month to make the cost worth it? Because for most local businesses in [city], ranking on Google for [their main service] brings in more than that. I can also structure payment in installments if that helps."

### "We'll think about it."
"Of course — no pressure. Can I share one example of a site I built for a similar business in [city/industry], so you have something concrete to look at? That might make the decision easier."

## Follow-Up Sequence

```
Day 1:  Initial pitch
Day 4:  Follow-up — "Did you get a chance to see my message? Happy to share an example."
Day 10: Value add — send a screenshot of a competitor's Google ranking with their business absent
Day 20: Final — "No worries if the timing isn't right — feel free to reach out whenever it makes sense."
```

Never follow up more than 3 times. If no response after Day 20, move on.

| Gym / yoga studio | ₹20k–₹50k | $300–$700 | Class schedule + membership |
| Clinic / dental / physio | ₹25k–₹60k | $400–$900 | Credibility + appointment |
| Boutique retail | ₹25k–₹70k | $400–$1,000 | Catalogue + Instagram integration |
| Event photographer | ₹20k–₹50k | $300–$700 | Portfolio + contact |
| Tutor / coaching center | ₹20k–₹45k | $300–$650 | Courses + testimonials + contact |

Present as a range, not a fixed number. Let them anchor high.
